# Greenlight Review Framework

Review concept, audience promise, episode engine, budget risk, production readiness, and release plan.

## When To Use This

Use this guide when the team needs a repeatable way to evaluate tools, workflows, content assets, or production decisions.

## Decision Areas

- Primary user intent
- Required inputs and constraints
- Workflow or production fit
- Quality, risk, and limitation notes
- Review owner and refresh cadence
- Next action after the review

## Practical Checklist

- The decision goal is written in one sentence.
- The inputs are clear enough for another reviewer to understand.
- Fit and non-fit scenarios are both documented.
- Any claims that need verification are marked clearly.
- The next action is specific.
- The review can be updated when product or workflow facts change.

## Common Mistakes

- Writing promotional notes instead of decision criteria.
- Comparing workflows without a real use case.
- Ignoring pricing, limits, rights, platform rules, or workflow constraints.
- Forgetting to preserve why a decision was made.
